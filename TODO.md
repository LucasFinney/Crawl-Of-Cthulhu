## RPG Systems

- [ ] **Lore stat/skill**: A knowledge stat (possibly profession-linked) that gates creature identification. Currently, killing one of a type adds it to the bestiary. With a Lore system, mundane creatures (rats, cultists) would be auto-recognized, but eldritch creatures (nightgaunts, shoggoths) would require sufficient Lore to *name* even after a kill. The Occultist would have high starting Lore; the Detective would need to build it up through play (finding journals, Necronomicon pages, etc.). Ties into readable lore items and profession differentiation. Makes more sense once the bestiary is larger.

### New Monster Ideas

- [ ] **Byakhee** — Flying servitors of Hastur. New "swooper" behavior: stays far away (like stalker), then dashes in for a hit-and-run attack, retreating immediately after striking. Forces the player to time shots carefully or waste ammo on a moving target. Could ignore some terrain (fly over water/pits). Mid-tier threat, appears around level 4-5.

- [ ] **Hunting Horror** — Nyarlathotep's serpentine flyers. "Darkseeker" behavior: drawn to darkness — moves faster and hits harder when the player's torch is off or low. If torch is lit, it keeps distance and hisses. Creates a real tension between conserving torch fuel (important for FOV) and keeping this thing at bay. The player who's been hoarding fuel suddenly has to burn it. High-tier, level 6+.

- [ ] **Colour Out of Space** — An alien entity that infects terrain. "Spreader" behavior: each turn it "taints" adjacent floor tiles (visual change). Tainted tiles drain SAN when walked on. Doesn't chase — just slowly contaminates an area, cutting off corridors and forcing detours. Killing it cleanses tiles. The decision: do you fight it now (spending HP/ammo) or route around it (spending time and potentially getting cornered)? Mid-tier, level 4+.

- [ ] **Serpent Person** (Yig's children) — Disguised as a cultist glyph/color until within melee range, then reveals as much stronger. Punishes careless melee against "easy" cultists on deeper floors. The player learns to be suspicious — do you waste a bullet to be safe, or risk walking up to what might be a serpent person? Level 3+.

- [ ] **Hound of Tindalos** — Extradimensional predator. "Phaser" behavior: can teleport to any tile the player has recently occupied (follows their path with a delay). Appears behind you. Immune to normal de-aggro — once it locks on, it doesn't stop. The only answer is to kill it or keep moving. Creates urgency and punishes backtracking. High-tier, level 5+.

- [ ] **Dark Young of Shub-Niggurath** — Massive tree-like horror. Takes up visual space (rendered as multiple glyphs?). Extremely slow (moves every other turn) but blocks corridors and has very high HP. "Waller" behavior: actively moves to block doorways and escape routes. The horror isn't its damage — it's being trapped between it and something else. Forces the player to think about positioning and escape routes. High-tier, level 6+.

- [ ] **Elder Thing** — Ancient and intelligent. "Adaptive" behavior: changes tactics based on what the player does. If shot at, it takes cover behind walls. If the player melees, it tries to maintain range. If the player runs, it cuts off escape routes by predicting movement. Essentially forces the player to vary their approach. Very dangerous because your usual strategy won't work. High-tier, level 7+.

- [ ] **Rat Thing** (Brown Jenkin) — Tiny, fast, hard to hit. Speed 2 (like Mi-Go) but with very low HP. Appears in packs of 2-3. Individually weak, but they steal items from your inventory on hit (drops the item on a nearby tile). Suddenly that spare ammo or medkit is on the floor behind a swarm of rats. Creates frantic scrambles and inventory anxiety. Low-mid tier, level 2+.

- [ ] **Formless Spawn** (of Tsathoggua) — Amorphous ooze. "Mimic" behavior: disguises itself as a floor item (ammo, medkit, etc.). When the player tries to pick it up, it attacks with surprise damage. After reveal, it's a slow but tough melee fighter. Punishes greedy item-grabbing and rewards cautious play (checking with ranged attacks, or noticing items in suspicious locations). Mid-tier, level 3+.

- [ ] **Fire Vampire** (of Cthugha) — "Volatile" behavior: explodes on death, dealing area damage to everything within 2 tiles (including other monsters). The player must decide: shoot it from range (safe but costs ammo), melee it and tank the explosion, or lure it near other monsters and use the explosion tactically. Low HP but the death burst is the real mechanic. Mid-tier, level 4+.

- [ ] **Moon-Beast** — Slavers from the Dreamlands. "Enslaver" behavior: doesn't attack directly. Instead, it buffs and commands nearby monsters, increasing their speed and aggression. Killing it returns other monsters to normal. Creates target prioritization decisions — do you fight through the buffed minions to reach the moon-beast, or try to pick off minions first while they're stronger? Mid-tier, level 5+.
